About Us

Artpreneurship was developed to help all artists, creatives, and designers to start or sustain a business, whether as a sole inventor, contributing innovator, or as an equal partner when developing a new product or service. With proven curriculums and support from collaborators, the goal of Artpreneurship is to create Artpreneurs who will use their thinking, curiosity, and talents to enhance society by developing products and services that improve human lives.

Donation & Sponsorship

Our Goal

Is to support artists, creators, and designers in their pursuit to create sustainable business practices, which will allow for personal and professional growth. Students will explore opportunities and learn how to solve problems by integrating design thinking, design sprints, and other creative processes. Students will design business strategies based on validated assumptions, using the feedback loop: design, measure, and learn.

  • Students will be encouraged to attend a series of lectures, workshops, and seminars to better familiarize themselves with business and business applications.
  • Students will tour and visit various businesses and will have an opportunity to learn and share their business models.
  • Students will be introduced to different levels of business development.

Curricula

The following is a short outline for new businesses. And there are three tiers of potential curriculums we can build.

Through these hands-on curriculum options, students will start, refine, or expand their work for an entrepreneurial venture.

Idealist
First is the Idealist. These are individuals who may have a dream of opening up a new business, but they really don’t have many resources or knowledge about how to start a new business.
Start-Ups
The second group is a pre-existing group of “Start-Ups.” They have some knowledge about business, but they need additional help.
Owners
The last group is comprised of existing business owners who simply want to expand or make their companies more sustainable.

Cooperation

The Artpreneurship Program will complement and align with the efforts of private and nonprofit organizations. We believe in sharing and working together to provide those individuals in the arts and humanities with opportunities to continue to explore and learn. Several organizations, including Toolbox LA, Bixel Exchange, LACI, and many programs out of CSUN will be cooperating to share ideas, approaches, resources, and best practices that offer students the best chance for success. If any individuals or organizations are interested in cooperative opportunities, please contact us.
